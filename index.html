Analogous colors are a group of three colors next to each other on a color wheel, the first is the dominant color, the second is the supporting color, and the third serves as an accent. The 60-30-10 rule is a good guide to follow. 60% dominant color, 30% supporting color, and 10% accent color. Analogous colors are often found in nature and are harmonious and pleasing to the eye. When these colors are grouped together it is called an analogous color scheme.
